Linking course sections

Before we begin, you will want to link the concurrent sections of your courses together. This will save some headache when setting up your grade book, it will also allow for shortcuts when you are doing your grading.

  • Detailed Instructions
  • Video Instructions

First from the Course tab go to See All. Click on the settings symbol () for the course section want to be linked. Then the choose other sections to be linked.

Caution

Only link sections at the beginning of the year. When sections are linked, data from one of the sections will be lost. Never unlink sections mid year, as gradebook data will be lost with for the linked section.

Once linked, materials added to the course will be added to all sections. There are options to make section specific material.

Building a folder structure

You may want to organize a previous years content, then move it to your new course A well organized materials section for your course will help students to navigate the course content.

Standard Expectation

All courses must have a Course Outline at the top of the materials. This will help parents in understanding what information to find where. Although different classes will understanbly have different folder structures, this document should be considered the best practice.

Best Practice

***Image of a standard folder stucture***

  • --- Course Outline ---
  • --- Unit 1: Title ---
    • --- Resources ---
    • --- Extensions ---
    • --- Review ---
  • --- Unit 2: Title ---
  • --- Unit 3: Title ---
  • --- Unit 4: Title ---
  • --- Assessments ---
  • --- Homework Assignments---
*** ---------------------------- ***

*** Image of Assessments folder ***
You may choose to place your assessments within the designated unit or accumulate them within on folder. The placement of your assessments will have no effect on how students and parents see their grade book (see gradebook settings)

*** Image of Homewok Assignments folde ***
Homework assignments help students track their daily work. These assignments can build up quickly and usually don't need to have a lot of content in them. Any time something needs to be submitted, or refered to later, it is a better practice to make these resources within a different folder that would be easier to find.
